TikTok vs Instagram ROI: which one wins for small businesses?

By SocialROI.tools · Updated January 2025 · 8 min read

TikTok and Instagram are both short-video platforms, but they work very differently for small business ROI. TikTok's algorithm can take an account with zero followers and put a video in front of 50,000 people overnight. Instagram's algorithm almost always favors established accounts. This single difference changes the ROI math dramatically — especially for new businesses.

When TikTok wins on ROI

TikTok delivers better ROI in two specific scenarios:

1. New businesses with no existing audience

On Instagram, a new account posting daily might reach 50–100 people per post. On TikTok, the same content can reach thousands from day one because the algorithm distributes content based on interest, not follower count. For a business starting from scratch, TikTok's time-to-first-lead is significantly faster.

2. Businesses where entertainment value is high

Restaurants showing food prep, contractors showing dramatic before/after, trainers showing workout transformations — any business where the process is visually interesting tends to see explosive organic reach on TikTok. The platform rewards content that people watch to completion and share.

When Instagram wins on ROI

Higher purchase intent

Instagram users are more accustomed to making purchases directly from the platform. Instagram Shopping, product tags, and the general expectation that businesses sell on Instagram create higher purchase intent than TikTok, where the primary expectation is entertainment.

Better for local businesses

Instagram's geotag and location features are more mature than TikTok's. A local business can reliably target their geographic area through location tags, local hashtags, and Instagram's paid local targeting. TikTok's algorithm is less predictable for geographic targeting.

Easier ad management

Instagram ads (run through Meta's ad platform) allow you to start with as little as $5/day and have some of the most precise demographic and interest targeting available. TikTok ads require a minimum of $50/day, making them impractical for most small businesses testing the waters.

The real answer: time cost

TikTok content generally takes longer to produce than Instagram content. A polished TikTok video with music, text overlays, and good pacing might take 45–60 minutes to produce. An Instagram Reel of comparable quality takes 20–30 minutes. At $50/hour, that's an extra $12–25 per piece of content — which compounds significantly over a month of posting.

This is why running both platforms simultaneously rarely makes sense for small businesses without a content team. Pick one, go deep, track the ROI, then consider the second.

The verdict for 2025

For new businesses or businesses in entertainment-friendly industries: start with TikTok. The organic reach advantage is too significant to ignore, and it can generate leads faster with zero ad spend.

For established local businesses or those with any ad budget: Instagram typically delivers more reliable, trackable ROI — especially if your customers are over 35.

Is TikTok or Instagram better for small businesses in 2025?

It depends on your industry and audience age. TikTok is better for discovery and new businesses with no audience. Instagram is better for local businesses, older demographics, and businesses with a small ad budget. Run our calculator with your actual numbers for both platforms to get a definitive answer.

Can I use both TikTok and Instagram for my business?

Yes, but only if you repurpose content. Create one video, post it on both platforms. Don't try to create separate native content for each — the time cost will hurt your ROI on both. Remove TikTok watermarks before posting to Instagram Reels (Instagram's algorithm suppresses watermarked content).

Which platform has better ROI for paid ads?

Instagram (via Meta ads) wins for paid ROI at small budgets — you can start at $5/day. TikTok requires $50/day minimum and works better for businesses spending $1,000+/month on ads. For most small businesses, Meta ads deliver better paid ROI.
